# Module 03 — Define

**Days 11–15** · ~3 hours total

---

## What this module earns you

By the end of Day 15, you will be able to move from a pile of raw empathy data to a sharp, actionable "How Might We" question — and you will have drilled that synthesis skill under exam conditions. This is the hardest module in the course. The Define phase is where most DT projects stall, because synthesis is genuinely difficult: it requires you to hold many observations simultaneously, resist the pull toward premature conclusions, and commit to a single problem framing that is specific enough to design against.

Day 11 is a consolidation day — no new material, only retrieval. Day 15 is a drill day — no new material, only synthesis practice. The three content days (12, 13, 14) teach the three tools of Define in sequence: insight → POV → HMW.
