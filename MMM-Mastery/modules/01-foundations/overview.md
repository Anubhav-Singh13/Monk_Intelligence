# Module 1 — Foundations

**Days 1–6 · ~3.5 hours**

## What This Module Earns You

By Day 6 you can look at any MMM output — a decomposition pie chart, a media ROI table, a coefficient report — and say three things with confidence:

1. **What it is claiming** — which sales are attributed to which driver, and on what assumptions
2. **What it cannot claim** — where attribution ends and causation would need to begin
3. **How the data got there** — which numbers came from Nielsen, which from internal systems, and why the two are joined the way they are

You also have the two core data transformations (adstock, saturation) in your hands as working concepts, and the 5Ps framework as the taxonomy that organises every driver you will encounter for the rest of the course.

## The Story of This Module

Marketing mix modelling has been sold, at various points, as a machine that turns spend data into optimal budget recommendations. This module begins by refusing that sale. MMM is a decomposition tool — a sophisticated way of asking "which variables move together with sales, and by how much?" That question is valuable. But it is a different question from "what would sales be if we changed our price?" or "did our TV campaign cause the sales uplift?"

The reason to learn this distinction on Day 1, before any mathematics, is that the distinction determines every modelling decision you will make. Whether you need an instrumental variable, whether a geographic test is warranted, whether you can trust a coefficient — all of it flows from understanding what the model is actually doing when it fits a line through data.

The remaining five days of this module give you the concrete vocabulary: what base sales and incremental sales mean and why their ratio matters for budget decisions; how advertising effects persist over time and why the spend variable in your regression cannot be raw weekly spend; how the relationship between spend and response saturates and what that means for ROI at different investment levels; and finally, the 5Ps framework that maps every lever a Unilever brand controls to a slot in the model.
