# Module 2 — The 5Ps as Drivers

**Days 7–12 · ~3.5 hours**

## What This Module Earns You

By Day 12 you can look at any Unilever brand's marketing mix and translate each lever into a model variable — knowing exactly what data source to use, what transformation (if any) to apply, what sign to expect on the coefficient, and where the estimation will be hard. You will have encountered every major driver type before the causal arc begins, so that Module 3's machinery solves problems you already recognise rather than abstract ones.

## The Story of This Module

Module 1 gave you the map. Module 2 walks the territory. Each of the five pages (Days 7–11) asks the same sequence of questions about one P: What is the business mechanism? What does it look like in data? What does the coefficient mean? Where does estimation break down?

The module ends on Day 12 with a Rest & Synthesize day — not because the content is hard, but because the 5Ps must be clear as a *taxonomy* before Module 3 uses them as a *diagnostic*. When a DAG on Day 14 shows that Price is endogenous, you need to already know what Price means in the model. When Day 17 finds an instrument for Price, you need to know what Price elasticity is supposed to estimate.

Brands rotate across the module to reflect the genuine differences between categories:
- **Surf Excel** anchors Price, Promotion, and Pack — a price-sensitive, promotionally active home care brand
- **Knorr** anchors Place — a distribution-driven, foodservice-facing food brand
- **Dove** anchors People — a premium personal care brand where execution quality is visible in account-level data
