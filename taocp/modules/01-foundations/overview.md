# Module 1 — Foundations

## What this module earns you

By the end of Day 8, you will hold three instruments Knuth uses on every page:

1. **Step counting** — the ability to measure an algorithm's work in a machine-independent way.
2. **Asymptotic naming** — the vocabulary to compare algorithms by how their costs *grow*, not by how fast any particular computer runs them.
3. **Invariant reasoning** — the technique of proving an algorithm correct by identifying a condition that survives every iteration.

Without these three, every subsequent module is pattern-memorisation. With them, you are reading Knuth the way he intended: as someone who can follow the argument, not just admire the conclusion.

## What to watch for

- **Day 1–2:** Slow down. Notation feels pedantic until it pays off in Day 3. Resist the urge to skim.
- **Day 4 (MIX):** You do not need to become a MIX programmer. You need to know enough to read sentences like "this instruction costs 2u." Treat it like learning just enough of a foreign language to read a menu.
- **Day 5:** The invariant idea is the single most important concept in the entire course. If Day 5 feels obvious, you have understood it. If it feels abstract, re-read the hook section before continuing.
- **Day 7:** Do not skip the rest day. Re-derivation is where understanding consolidates.
